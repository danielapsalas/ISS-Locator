What is the International Space station(ISS)?

The ISS, is a vast spacecraft and space station, that orbits the Earth at about 250 miles. It acts as a distinctive laboratory for scientific research, fostering international collaboration and space technology progress. Scientists from various space agencies conduct experiments, allowing advances in numerous scientific fields.

Fun Facts:

  1. In just one day, the space station covers a distance equivalent to a round trip to the Moon.
  2. The International Space Station (ISS) stands as the most massive human-made structure ever launched into space.
  3. The ISS has had to change its orbit/position to evade space debris, ensuring a safe journey among the stars.
  4. Mark Vande Hei's extraordinary ISS mission lasted 355 days, breaking the previous record of 340 days set by Scott Kelly on March 1, 2016.
  5. NASA announced that the space station's operations will end in 2030, and it will eventually fall into the Pacific Ocean.
